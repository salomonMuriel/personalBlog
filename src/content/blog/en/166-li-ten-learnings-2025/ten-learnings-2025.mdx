10 learnings from 2025 🔽

1. It's worth talking to people. Lots of people. My 107 conversations from January to April to research Ignia were the foundation for every step that followed. Thank you [Santiago Amador](https://www.linkedin.com/in/santiago-amador-91b1733b) for being the first.

2. Posting every day actually works. [Jose Manuel Prieto](https://www.linkedin.com/in/josemprieto1) has it figured out.

3. Building community rocks 🤘 how awesome to meet new people every single week. Thank you [Sebastian  Eljaiek](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sebastian-eljaiek) for creating the Remote Meetups.

4. Claude Code is the real deal. You can genuinely produce 40X more code at quality using it — and it's not just for programming.

5. You have to listen to other people's opinions no matter where they come from, and take the best you can from them with respect (or argue them with respect too). Ideally without resorting to any scatology 💩

6. You can innovate in any context — public or private, big or small. [Natalia Rodríguez Triana](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nat-innovacion) best teacher ever 🚀

7. You can build profitable companies from day one. Also profitable + impactful. Also dreaming big. Thank you [Camilo Bonilla](https://www.linkedin.com/in/camilobonilla) for showing me how.

8. It's possible to run a half marathon (or any other goal) even if you've NEVER done it before. It's just 1) get advice, 2) prepare properly, and 3) take the leap. Thank you [Nicolás Varón](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nicolasvaronrodriguez) for the final push 🏃‍♂️

9. It's hard as hell but achievable to be a present dad and maintain balance while building a company at the same time. Thank you Franco and Luca for the patience and the adventures ❤️

10. A lesson I forget and then remember — keep a positive face through the rough times. And through the good times, and the mediocre ones too. Optimism above all, especially in these days of not-great news. Here's to an incredible 2026 for everyone 🚀

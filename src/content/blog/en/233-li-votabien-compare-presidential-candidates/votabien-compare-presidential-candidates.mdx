Getting informed about the candidates' proposals was a nightmare (I couldn't even find 2 of them!!) — so I built a portal to fix it 🤓

[Votabien.co](http://Votabien.co) — Compare the proposals, backgrounds, and positions of all candidates in one place. Every statement right next to its source.

Together with my friend Claude, I pulled the positions and proposals for the top 5 candidates across 10 macro topics and 14 current issues.

With as much transparency as possible — everything cited, links to sources, the same prompts used to research everyone, order randomly shuffled throughout the day so no one gets extra visibility. The goal is to inform, not to convince or influence.

Let me know what you think, what you'd add (or remove), if you spot anything incorrectly cited, if you think it actually helps or is maybe just a waste of time. I got so excited about this one I even bought a domain 🙈 but I know it has a lot of room to improve.

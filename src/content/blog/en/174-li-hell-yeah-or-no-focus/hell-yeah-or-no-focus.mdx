We need to say no more often. The best method I've seen: Hell Yeah 🤩 or No ❌

I saw a post from [Felipe Novoa](https://www.linkedin.com/in/felipenovoaufk) about how so many entrepreneurs struggle to focus and stick with one thing — we love starting new stuff.

It happens to me constantly — I want to be everywhere. Yes to every project, every speaking invitation. I'll figure out where to find the time later.

Last year, [Mauricio Salazar Velez](https://www.linkedin.com/in/mauriciosalazarvelez) very respectfully told me I'd moved past the "trying to do everything" phase.

I get it now, Mauricio — you have to be really intentional about where you invest your time.

And that means saying no to most things that come your way — like 99% of them. Easier said than done.

▶️ The tactic: if something is a "Hell Yeah 🔥" (meaning UNMISSABLE, AMAZING, SPECTACULAR), say yes.

Everything else? Say no.

I found it in Feel-Good Productivity, but the original credit apparently goes to Derek Sivers, who I randomly crossed paths with about a year ago.

It makes complete sense to me. If something doesn't feel incredible, it's probably pulling me away from what matters most.

Starting to apply this year — let's see if I feel less overwhelmed, if I can keep fewer balls from dropping each week.

What do you think? What other tactics do you use to "protect" your time?

These are my old startup awards — and today they give me zero pride 🫠

When I won these (plus another one that broke), my companies weren't profitable. And were never going to be.

I won them with talk — standing in front of crowds, spreading emotion and dreams.

But behind it all, there wasn't much — an idea, some technology (pretty thin, honestly), little to no revenue.

I won them with smoke 🌪️. Selling smoke, spreading smoke.

I'm not proud of selling smoke anymore. I don't go to events to chase awards.

I'd rather earn the prize of having a profitable company — the trophy doesn't matter anymore.

And there's nothing wrong with entering competitions and going to events — they give visibility and market validation. I might go back someday.

But I really hope these awards go to companies with solid metrics behind them — not just great ideas and great dream-sellers.

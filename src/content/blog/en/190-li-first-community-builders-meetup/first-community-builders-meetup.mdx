Yesterday was Bogotá's first Community Builders Meetup 🤩

A meta-community — a community of community creators.

An idea born out of nowhere, from a quick chat with [Tim Bonnemann](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tbonnemann) and [Adriana Portilla Llaña](https://www.linkedin.com/in/adrianaportilla1).

And a LinkedIn post that went viral — 100+ sign-ups, and despite the DOWNPOUR yesterday, over 50 attendees.

It was awesome to see how it all unfolded. We had 3 five-minute talks planned, but it turned into an Open Mic about the art of bringing people together:

1. [Luis Betancourt](https://www.linkedin.com/in/luisbetancourt) on the community lifecycle.
2. Me showing how to use tech to manage an in-person community.
3. [Tim Bonnemann](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tbonnemann) sharing his 20 years as a Community Architect.

🪄 And the magic of organic — without any planning, several people stood up to share:

4. [Rogert Ovalle](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rogertovalle), the first brave soul, sharing how he's seen many communities be born and die.
5. [Carolina Laiton Galán](https://www.linkedin.com/in/carolinalaiton) on the inverted funnel in communities.
6. [Santiago Grisales](https://www.linkedin.com/in/santiago-grisales-bohórquez) on where things go sideways 💩 when managing a community.
7. [Valeria Soler](https://www.linkedin.com/in/valeriasoler) on connecting with our purpose.

So many new connections. Meeting faces that only existed virtually. Incredible.

We agreed to do it again in 2 months, in March. We'll let you know.

What tools, frameworks, or experiences do you have with building communities? How do you picture a future gathering? I really want to do an Unconference. Who's in?

Colombia's EdTech ecosystem is at an inflection point 📈 Why?

1. Community Consolidation ➡️ [Colombia EdTech](https://www.linkedin.com/company/edtechhublatam/) recently held its first assembly. 50+ companies discussing shared challenges in applying technology to education. The new leadership of [Andrés Méndez](https://linkedin.com/in/andresfmendez) strongly echoes the early takeoff of [Colombia Fintech](https://www.linkedin.com/company/colombiafintech/) under [Edwin Zácipa 🚀](https://linkedin.com/in/edwinzacipa) and [Erick Rincon Cardenas](https://linkedin.com/in/erick-rincon-cardenas-06134248), or [Colombia Proptech](https://www.linkedin.com/company/colombia-proptech/) with [Shirlhey Sanchez](https://linkedin.com/in/shirlhey-sanchez-aa763118).

2. Mature Players ➡️ Companies like [Scala Learning](https://www.linkedin.com/company/scalalearningeducation/), [Coschool](https://www.linkedin.com/company/coschoolcol/), [UBITS](https://www.linkedin.com/school/ubitslearning/), and [Platzi](https://www.linkedin.com/school/platzi-inc/) prove it's possible to build the market and sustain an organization long-term. Behind them comes the "second wave" that will give even more relevance to the vertical.

3. Advantages in Capital Scarcity ➡️ Education is challenging because of its difficult scalability, so many models (even tech-enabled ones) look more like Camels 🐫 (steady but not explosive growth) and Zebras 🦓 (profit-impact balance) than Unicorns 🦄. In a VC capital scarcity scenario, these more measured growth models have the advantage.

The wave is coming 🌊 — across tech-services ([Edupleia](https://www.linkedin.com/company/edupleia/), [Acreditta](https://www.linkedin.com/company/acreditta/)), tech-program-delivery ([LaTeacher](https://www.linkedin.com/company/lateacher/), [ADA School](https://www.linkedin.com/school/adaschool/)), tech-enabled-programs ([Arukay](https://www.linkedin.com/company/arukay/), [Ignia](https://www.linkedin.com/company/igniaeducation/)), and more.
